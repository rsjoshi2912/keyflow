05 · Scoring

Numbers you can trust

TypeAce uses standard, exam-grade WPM formulas — the same math is unit-tested in the engine.

Net WPM

72 wpm

Correct characters ÷ 5 ÷ minutes. Only correctly typed words (and their spaces) count.

Accuracy

96.4 %

Correct keystrokes ÷ total keystrokes. Fixing a typo doesn’t erase the mistake.

Consistency

88 /100

100 minus the variation of your per-second speed. Steady beats bursty.

Gross WPM

78 wpm

All typed characters ÷ 5 ÷ minutes — your raw speed before errors.

06 · FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Everything people ask before downloading a free typing app.

Is TypeAce really free typing software?

Yes. TypeAce is 100% free and MIT-licensed. There are no ads, no premium tier, no account and no in-app purchases. You download the app once and everything — including all exam mock tests — is included.

Can I practice typing exams like SSC, CPCT or RRB with TypeAce?

Yes. TypeAce includes exam-pattern mock tests modeled on SSC CHSL DEST, SSC CGL DEST, CPCT English typing, RRB NTPC typing skill test and court exam patterns. Mocks replicate real exam rules — backspace disabled, continuous passage, fixed duration — and give a clear qualified / not-qualified verdict. Always verify the latest official notification for exact rules.

Does TypeAce work offline?

Yes. TypeAce is a desktop app that works fully offline. Tests, mocks, lessons and your progress history all run and are stored locally on your computer — nothing is sent to the internet.

Which platforms does TypeAce support?

TypeAce runs on macOS (both Apple Silicon M1/M2/M3/M4 and Intel Macs) and on Windows 10 and Windows 11 (64-bit). Separate installers are provided for each above.

How does TypeAce calculate typing speed (WPM)?

Net WPM is correct characters divided by 5, divided by minutes — only correctly typed words count. Gross WPM counts all typed characters. Accuracy is correct keystrokes over total keystrokes, and corrections don’t erase mistakes. The same formulas used by typing exams.

Why does my computer warn me when I first open TypeAce?

TypeAce builds are not yet code-signed, so macOS Gatekeeper and Windows SmartScreen show a one-time warning for apps from new developers. On macOS, right-click the app and choose Open. On Windows, click More info → Run anyway. You can verify your download against the published SHA-256 checksums.

I used KeyFlow — is TypeAce the same app?

Yes. KeyFlow was renamed TypeAce with version 1.0.0, alongside a full redesign and new features (exam mocks, per-key analytics, on-screen keyboard and more). Your existing progress is migrated automatically the first time you open TypeAce on the same computer.
