Scoring

Typing speed numbers you can trust

KeyFlow uses standard, exam-grade WPM formulas — the same math is unit-tested in the engine.

Net WPM

72 wpm

Correct characters ÷ 5 ÷ minutes. Only correctly typed words (and their spaces) count.

Accuracy

96.4 %

Correct keystrokes ÷ total keystrokes. Fixing a typo doesn’t erase the mistake.

Consistency

88 /100

100 minus the variation of your per-second speed. Steady beats bursty.

Gross WPM

78 wpm

All typed characters ÷ 5 ÷ minutes — your raw speed before errors.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Everything people ask before downloading a free typing app.

Is KeyFlow really free typing software?

Yes. KeyFlow is 100% free and MIT-licensed. There are no ads, no premium tier, no account and no in-app purchases. You download the app once and everything is included.

Does KeyFlow work offline?

Yes. KeyFlow is a desktop app that works fully offline. Tests, lessons and your progress history all run and are stored locally on your computer — nothing is sent to the internet.

Which platforms does KeyFlow support?

KeyFlow runs on macOS (both Apple Silicon M1/M2/M3/M4 and Intel Macs, macOS 10.12 or later) and on Windows 10 and Windows 11 (64-bit). Separate installers are provided for each above.

Can I learn touch typing from scratch with KeyFlow?

Yes. KeyFlow includes a 10-step lesson course that starts at the home row and builds up to full-alphabet words. Each lesson unlocks when you reach 20 WPM at 90% accuracy, so fundamentals stick before you move on.

How does KeyFlow calculate typing speed (WPM)?

Net WPM is correct characters divided by 5, divided by minutes — only correctly typed words count. Gross WPM counts all typed characters. Accuracy is correct keystrokes over total keystrokes, and corrections don’t erase mistakes. The same formulas used by typing exams.

Why does my computer warn me when I first open KeyFlow?

Early KeyFlow builds are not yet code-signed, so macOS Gatekeeper and Windows SmartScreen show a one-time warning for apps from new developers. On macOS, right-click the app and choose Open. On Windows, click More info → Run anyway. You can verify your download against the published SHA-256 checksums.
